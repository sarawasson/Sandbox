Research

I am a Reader in English Literature in the School of Arts at Lancaster University. I specialise critical in medical and environmental humanities, slow violence, chronic pain, transplantation studies, and critical plant studies. I have published extensively in Gothic studies and critical medical humanities in journals such as the BMJ Medical Humanities¸ the journal Literature and Medicine hosted at Johns Hopkins University, Gothic Studies, and the Journal of Popular Culture. My most recent monograph, Transplantation Gothic: Tissue Transfer in Literature, Film and Medicine(Manchester: Manchester University Press, 2020) won the International Gothic Association’s Allan Lloyd Smith Award in 2022 and was short-listed for the British Society for Literature and Science Award in 2021.

I was Primary Investigator for the UK AHRC-funded interdisciplinary project Translating Chronic Pain (2017-2019) and have developed it into an ongoing international online platform with work from 13 countries and visitors to date from 103 countries. I am also a creative writer of flash on ecological themes.

I am currently working on a book for Cambridge University Press entitled Medical Gothic and Bodies Dis(re)membered (forthcoming in their Elements series in 2026). I am also working on creative writing informed by Critical Plant Studies, honouring ancient temperate oakwood biomes of North Wales.
